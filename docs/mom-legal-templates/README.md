# Legal Document Templates for Mom

**IMPORTANT DISCLAIMER:** These are templates/starting points only and do NOT
constitute legal advice. Laws vary significantly by state. For these documents
to be legally valid, they generally must:

- Comply with your specific state's laws
- Be signed in the presence of witnesses (usually 2, who are NOT beneficiaries)
- Be notarized (required in some states, recommended in all)
- The person signing must be of sound mind and acting voluntarily

**Strongly recommended:** Have an attorney review the final versions before
signing. Many attorneys offer flat-fee packages for basic estate planning
(will + POA + advance directive) that are very affordable ($300-$800 range).

Some states also offer free or low-cost legal aid for estate planning.

## Next Steps

1. Mom should decide which STATE's laws apply (where she lives/is domiciled)
2. Fill in the blanks in each template
3. Have an attorney review (or at minimum, research her state's specific requirements)
4. Sign with proper witnesses and notarization
5. Give copies to the people named in the documents
6. Keep originals in a safe, accessible place (NOT a safety deposit box - those can be sealed at death)
