# Mom's Advance Directive & DNR — Pre-Filled Draft

**This document handles the "who's in charge of medical decisions" and "do
not resuscitate" questions mom mentioned.**

It has two parts that work together:

- **Part A — Healthcare Power of Attorney:** Names the person who speaks for
  mom if she can't speak for herself
- **Part B — Living Will:** States her specific wishes (including DNR)

### 2. Powers Granted to My Agent

My Agent is authorized to:

- Consent to, refuse, or withdraw any medical treatment, procedure, or surgery
- Access all of my medical records (this includes **HIPAA authorization**)
- Choose my doctors, hospitals, and care facilities
- Move me between facilities as needed
- Authorize or refuse pain medication
- Make decisions about life-sustaining treatment consistent with Part B below
- Authorize organ/tissue donation consistent with my wishes in Part B
- Make decisions about autopsy and disposition of my remains

### 3. When My Agent's Authority Begins

My Agent's authority takes effect when my attending physician determines that
I am unable to make or communicate my own healthcare decisions. It ends if I
regain that ability.

## PART B — LIVING WILL (MY SPECIFIC WISHES)

### 4. When These Wishes Apply

The instructions below apply if I am in **any** of the following conditions,
as determined by my attending physician and confirmed by a second physician:

- A **terminal condition** (incurable, expected to cause death soon)
- A **permanent unconscious state** (persistent vegetative state or
  irreversible coma)
- An **end-stage medical condition** where recovery to a meaningful quality
  of life is not possible

### 5. Do Not Resuscitate (DNR)

☐ **If my heart stops or I stop breathing under the conditions in Section 4,
I DO NOT want cardiopulmonary resuscitation (CPR), defibrillation, or
intubation. I want a DO NOT RESUSCITATE order.**

*(Mom: check the box if this is your wish. Leave unchecked if you want CPR
attempted.)*

**⚠️ Important:** For paramedics to honor a DNR in an emergency at home,
you typically also need a separate **POLST or MOLST form** signed by your
doctor. Ask her primary care doctor at her next visit — they fill it out in
about 10 minutes. That's the form that rides on the fridge and EMTs actually
follow.

### 6. Life-Sustaining Treatment

If I am in one of the conditions in Section 4, my wishes for life-sustaining
treatment are:

☐ **I do NOT want** life-sustaining treatments that merely prolong the
dying process. I want to be allowed to die naturally, with comfort care only.

☐ I want life-sustaining treatment for a **trial period**, after which my
Agent should reassess based on whether I'm improving.

☐ **I DO want** all available life-sustaining treatments, regardless of
prognosis.

*(Mom: check one.)*

### 7. Specific Treatments

Even under the conditions in Section 4, my wishes for specific treatments are:

| Treatment | I do NOT want | I DO want | Agent decides |
|---|:---:|:---:|:---:|
| CPR / resuscitation | ☐ | ☐ | ☐ |
| Mechanical ventilator (breathing machine) | ☐ | ☐ | ☐ |
| Feeding tube (artificial nutrition) | ☐ | ☐ | ☐ |
| IV fluids (artificial hydration) | ☐ | ☐ | ☐ |
| Dialysis | ☐ | ☐ | ☐ |
| Antibiotics (except for comfort) | ☐ | ☐ | ☐ |
| Blood transfusions | ☐ | ☐ | ☐ |

### 8. Comfort Care — Always

**Regardless of any choice above, I ALWAYS want:**

- Pain medication and comfort measures, even if they may hasten death
- Cleanliness, hygiene, and dignity
- The presence of loved ones if possible
- Spiritual support if I have requested it

## AFTER SIGNING — DO THESE THINGS

1. **Give copies to:**
   - Her primary doctor (ask them to file it in her chart)
   - The named Healthcare Agent (you or sibling)
   - The alternate Agent
   - The local hospital if there's one she'd most likely go to
   - Keep an original at home somewhere findable

2. **Ask her doctor about a POLST/MOLST form** — this is the one-page
   medical order that paramedics actually follow. Put it on the fridge.

3. **Wallet card** — a small card noting "I have an Advance Directive" with
   the Agent's phone number is smart

4. **Review every few years** or after major life changes
