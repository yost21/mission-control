# Mom's Simple Will — Pre-Filled Draft

**A Last Will and Testament for a straightforward situation: everything split
50/50 between two adult children.**

This is tailored to Mom's wishes as expressed. Only a few personal details
need filling in (marked **[LIKE THIS]**). Highlighted in the checklist below.

---

## LAST WILL AND TESTAMENT

### OF **[MOM'S FULL LEGAL NAME]**

---

I, **[MOM'S FULL LEGAL NAME]**, residing at **[STREET ADDRESS]**,
**[CITY]**, **[COUNTY]**, **[STATE]**, being of sound mind and memory, and
not acting under duress or undue influence, do hereby make, publish, and
declare this to be my Last Will and Testament, hereby revoking all prior wills
and codicils made by me.

---

### ARTICLE I — FAMILY

I have two children, both of whom are living and are adults:

1. **[CHILD 1 FULL LEGAL NAME]**, born **[DATE OF BIRTH]**
2. **[CHILD 2 FULL LEGAL NAME]**, born **[DATE OF BIRTH]**

All references in this Will to "my children" refer to the two individuals
named above.

---

### ARTICLE II — PAYMENT OF DEBTS, EXPENSES, AND TAXES

I direct my Executor to pay from my estate, as soon as practicable after my
death:

1. My legally enforceable debts
2. The expenses of my last illness and funeral
3. The costs of administering my estate
4. Any estate or inheritance taxes owed

---

### ARTICLE III — ASSETS WITH NAMED BENEFICIARIES

I acknowledge that certain of my assets — including but not limited to bank
accounts, investment accounts, retirement accounts, and life insurance
policies — may have beneficiary designations on file with the relevant
institutions. **Those assets shall pass according to those beneficiary
designations and are NOT controlled by this Will.** This Will governs only
assets that do not have valid beneficiary designations.

---

### ARTICLE IV — RESIDUARY ESTATE (THE MAIN GIFT)

I give, devise, and bequeath all the rest, residue, and remainder of my
estate, of every kind and nature and wherever situated — including all real
property, personal property, household goods, vehicles, cash, and any other
assets not otherwise effectively disposed of — **in equal shares, fifty
percent (50%) each, to my two children**, namely:

- **[CHILD 1 FULL LEGAL NAME]** — 50%
- **[CHILD 2 FULL LEGAL NAME]** — 50%

**If one of my children predeceases me** (dies before me), that child's share
shall pass:

- [ ] *Option A:* to that child's descendants (my grandchildren), per stirpes
  (meaning divided equally among their children)
- [ ] *Option B:* to my surviving child
- [ ] *Option C:* **[OTHER - SPECIFY]**

*(Mom: pick one option above. Option A is most common.)*

---

### ARTICLE V — EXECUTOR

I appoint **[PRIMARY EXECUTOR NAME — often one of the kids]** to serve as
Executor (Personal Representative) of my estate.

If that person is unable or unwilling to serve, I appoint
**[ALTERNATE EXECUTOR NAME]** to serve as Executor.

My Executor:
- Shall serve **without bond** (or with the minimum bond required by law)
- Shall have all powers granted to executors under the laws of **[STATE]**,
  including the power to sell, lease, or distribute property; to settle
  claims; and to distribute the estate in cash or in kind
- Shall be entitled to reasonable compensation and reimbursement of expenses

---

### ARTICLE VI — PERSONAL PROPERTY MEMORANDUM (OPTIONAL)

I may leave a separate written list, signed and dated by me, directing the
distribution of specific items of tangible personal property (jewelry,
furniture, keepsakes, etc.). My Executor shall honor that list to the extent
permitted by the laws of **[STATE]**.

*(This is a nice option — lets mom update specific item gifts without
rewriting the will. Many states allow this.)*

---

### ARTICLE VII — GENERAL PROVISIONS

1. If any provision of this Will is invalid, the remaining provisions shall
   remain in full force and effect.
2. Pronouns shall be read in whichever gender or number the context requires.
3. This Will shall be governed by the laws of the State of **[STATE]**.

---

### ARTICLE VIII — NO-CONTEST CLAUSE

If any beneficiary contests this Will or any of its provisions, that
beneficiary shall forfeit their entire share, which shall be distributed as
though that beneficiary had predeceased me.

## WHAT MOM NEEDS TO FILL IN (THE SHORT LIST)

1. Her **full legal name** (as on driver's license)
2. Her **address** (street, city, county, state)
3. **Each kid's full legal name + date of birth**
4. Pick **Option A, B, or C** in Article IV (what happens if a kid dies first — Option A is the usual pick)
5. **Executor** — who runs the estate. Usually one of the kids, or whichever is more organized / willing.
6. **Alternate Executor** — backup in case the first can't serve
7. Page count on the signature page
8. Date of signing
9. **[STATE]** wherever it appears (fill in her state)

That's it. Everything else is standard language.

---

## WHAT HAPPENS NEXT (THE SIGNING DAY)

1. Print the final filled-in version
2. Gather **2 witnesses** (neighbors, coworkers, friends — **not the kids, not anyone inheriting**)
3. Bring in a **notary** (friend if she's commissioned, or a mobile notary — google "mobile notary near [city]", they'll come to the house, usually $25–75)
4. Everyone is in the room at the same time:
   - Mom signs the Will in front of the witnesses
   - Witnesses sign in front of mom and each other
   - Everyone signs the Self-Proving Affidavit in front of the notary
   - Notary stamps and signs
5. Store the original in a safe, findable place **at home** (NOT a safety deposit box — those can get sealed at death)
6. Give copies to the executor and each kid
